## 🎤 **Q&A PREPARATION (2 minutes)**

### **Expected Questions & Winning Answers**

#### **Q1: "How do you prevent the AI from hallucinating or giving wrong feedback?"**

**Answer:**

> "Great question. We have **3 layers of validation**:
>
> 1. **AgentDebate Protocol** — multiple agents vote on decisions, ensuring high-confidence outputs
> 2. **Dual LLM redundancy** — Gemini primary + Groq fallback. If one fails, the other takes over
> 3. **Knowledge base grounding** — our diagnostic questions are pre-validated against domain expertise
>
> Plus, every agent decision is logged with reasoning in our Explainability Console, so users can audit the system."

---

## 🎯 **PRESENTATION TIPS FOR WINNING**

### **Before You Present:**

1. **Practice the demo 10 times** — know exactly where to click, what to show
2. **Time yourself** — stay within 7 minutes (judges will cut you off)
3. **Memorize the hook** — first 30 seconds must be perfect
4. **Have backup plan** — if demo fails, have screenshots ready
5. **Dress professionally** — first impressions matter

### **During Presentation:**

1. **Speak with confidence** — you built something incredible
2. **Make eye contact** — connect with judges
3. **Use pauses** — let key points sink in
4. **Show enthusiasm** — your energy is contagious
5. **Handle tech issues calmly** — if demo breaks, pivot to slides

### **Body Language:**

- **Stand tall** — confident posture
- **Use hand gestures** — emphasize key points
- **Smile** — you're excited about this
- **Move naturally** — don't stand frozen
- **Point at screen** — guide judges' attention

### **Voice Control:**

- **Vary your pace** — slow down for important points
- **Emphasize key words** — "autonomous," "agentic," "adaptive"
- **Project your voice** — everyone should hear you
- **Avoid filler words** — no "um," "uh," "like"
